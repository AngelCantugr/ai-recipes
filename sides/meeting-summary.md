## Purpose
Create clear, actionable meeting summaries and distribute decisions

## When to Use
- After technical discussions
- Design review meetings
- Planning sessions
- Decision-making meetings

## Template

```
Help me create a clear summary of our meeting about [topic].

Meeting Details:
- Date: [when]
- Attendees: [who participated]
- Purpose: [why we met]

Discussion Points:
[Raw notes or key topics discussed]
- [Point 1]
- [Point 2]
- [Point 3]

Decisions Made:
[What was decided]

Action Items:
[Tasks that came out of the meeting]
- [Item 1]
- [Item 2]

Open Questions:
[What remains unresolved]

Please help me structure this into a clear summary:

1. **Executive Summary** (2-3 sentences)
   - Key outcomes in plain language
   - For people who need the gist

2. **Decisions**
   - What was decided
   - Rationale (briefly)
   - Who has authority to change

3. **Action Items**
   - What needs to be done
   - Who is responsible
   - When it's due
   - Dependencies

4. **Key Discussion Points**
   - Main topics covered
   - Important context shared
   - Concerns raised
   - Alternative approaches considered

5. **Open Issues**
   - What needs more discussion
   - Who will drive resolution
   - When we'll revisit

6. **Next Steps**
   - Follow-up meetings needed
   - Timeline for action items
   - How to track progress

Format for:
- Email distribution
- Documentation/wiki
- Slack/chat announcement

Ensure summary:
- Is clear and scannable
- Focuses on outcomes, not process
- Includes all commitments
- Addresses concerns raised
```

## Tips
- Send summary within 24 hours
- Highlight action items clearly
- Link to relevant documents
- Tag responsible people
- Keep it concise but complete
- Make decisions easy to find
- Track action items to completion
- Archive for future reference
